# My First Semester Journey

## Introduction to My Journey

Starting university is often described as an exciting new chapter—but for me, it began with uncertainty and emotional struggle.

### A Forced Shift: From Law to Computer Engineering

My academic journey didn’t begin with a clear passion for my chosen field. I had always dreamed of pursuing Law, a field that truly interested me. However, due to family concerns about its suitability for women, I wasn’t able to follow that path.

I then considered Civil Engineering as an alternative, hoping it would align better with my interests. Unfortunately, that option was also discouraged. Eventually, Computer Engineering became the only available choice left for me.

### Lack of Initial Interest

Accepting Computer Engineering wasn’t easy. I had no prior interest in computers, nor did I feel any connection to the field. Everything—from programming concepts to technical subjects—felt unfamiliar and overwhelming. It was difficult to find motivation in something I hadn’t chosen for myself.

### An Emotional Beginning

The start of my university life was emotionally challenging. Walking into this new phase with a sense of detachment made it harder to adjust. While others seemed excited about their chosen paths, I was still trying to come to terms with mine.
